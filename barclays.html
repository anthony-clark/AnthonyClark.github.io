Creating a website to highlight everything that the island of Guernsey has to offer

The project

I was the lead designer on the Visit Guernsey project, coming up with wireframes and designs for each template to present back to the client for sign off. The website needed to be strikingly visual and improve upon the key user journeys such as planning your stay or how to get to the island. It needed to gel with improving Visit Guernsey's own yearly targets and aims.

Deliverables

  • Wireframes
  • Visual design
  • Creative direction
  • Pattern library

Project aims

  • Improve the key user journeys across the website
  • Allow visitors to easily plan and research their stay
  • Use beautiful imagery to showcase Guernsey
  • Optimise templates to push Visit Guernsey's key targets
  • Showcase 'Guernsey's Great Things'
  • Allow visitors to browse via a map

The idea behind the landing page was to clearly signpost the different search options to the users. The page introduces two different ways to search for a car depending on whether you already know what you are after, or you need help in finding the right car. My initial idea was to have these options on the actual search page as tabs, but whilst I was wireframing the page I felt this made the search page cluttered and harder to follow.Â People could also miss the tabs so I decided to introduce a landing page which would require users to make a decision, and depending on their choice, we would show them the relevant search page.

The header also allows returning users to easily access their messages, saved cars and previous searches.
